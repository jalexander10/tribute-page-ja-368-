Sabrina Carpenter

American Singer and Actress

Career Timeline ~

1999-2024:

1999: Sabrina Annlynn Carpenter

was born on May 11th, 1999

2009: She started posting song covers on YouTube,

she also won 3rd place in Miley Cyrus’ “Be A Star”

contest, which was a singing competition

2011: Her first acting role was a guest role on the

NBC series “Law & Order: Special Victims Unit”

2014-2017: Sabrina starred on the Disney Channel

series Girl Meets World, she also signed with

Hollywood Records, which is owned by Disney

2014: She released her debut single and EP,

both titled “Can't Blame a Girl for Trying”

2015: She released her

debut studio album,

called “Eyes Wide Open”

2016: She released her

2nd studio album,

called “Evolution” which

debuted on the Billboard

200 at number 28

2016: She went on her first headlining concert

tour, which was called the “Evolution Tour”

2017: She went on her 2nd headlining concert

tour, the “De-Tour”, she also opened for Ariana

Grande’s “Dangerous Woman Tour” in São Paulo

2018: Sabrina released her 3rd studio album,

called “Singular: Act I”, originally meant to be

released as one album, but was split into 2 parts

2019: She went on her 3rd headlining concert

tour, the “Singular Tour”, she also released her

4th studio album titled “Singular: Act II”

2020: She made her Broadway debut in Mean Girls,

but unfortunately, the show closed less than a

month later because of the COVID-19 pandemic

2020: She was in the Forbes' 30

Under 30 list in the Hollywood

and entertainment category

2021: She announced that she

had signed with Universal Music

Group's Island Records

2021: She released a single,

called “Skin”, after signing

with the new record label, and

the song debuted at number 48 on the Billboard

Hot 100, becoming her first entry on the chart

2022: She released her 5th studio album, titled

“Emails I Can't Send”, which debuted at

number 23 on the Billboard 200

2022: The song "Nonsense" from

her album “Emails I Can't Send”

went viral on TikTok, peaked at

number 56 on the Hot 100, was

certified Platinum by the RIAA,

and reached the top-10 on the

US Pop Airplay chart

2022: Sabrina went on her 4th

headlining concert tour, called

the “Emails I Can’t Send Tour”

2023: She released a deluxe edition of “Emails I

Can't Send”, and one of the bonus tracks, called

“Feather” peaked at number 1 on the US Pop

Songs chart and number 21 on the Hot 100.

She performed the song at the pre-show

for the 2023 MTV Video Music Awards

2023-2024: She was the opening act for Taylor

Swift’s “Era’s Tour” in many international shows

2023: She released a Christmas

themed EP, titled “Fruitcake”

2024: Sabrina released her

single, called “Espresso” and

performed at the 23rd Coachella

Valley Music and Arts Festival.

The song topped the Billboard

Global 200, peaked at number 3

on the Billboard Hot 100 chart

and went on to win the MTV

Video Music Award for Song of the Year

2024: She released

another single, called

“Please Please Please”,

which became her 2nd

global chart-topper and

her first US Hot 100

number 1 single

2024: She became the first

female artist to hold the

number 1 and 2 positions

on the UK singles chart

for 3 weeks in a row

2024: Sabrina released her 6th studio album,

titled “Short n’ Sweet”, which debuted at number 1

on the US Billboard 200 chart. All songs from the

album charted above top 50 on the Hot 100!

2024: She started her first arena tour,

called the “Short n’ Sweet Tour”

“The present is our future past, we've gotta make this moment last”

-Sabrina Carpenter
